# Hackathon Playbook

TRIZ was built for physical/engineering contradictions, not business ones — so use it metaphorically.

1. State your product tension as an improving-thing vs. worsening-thing pair: "more personalization" vs. "less privacy," "faster checkout" vs. "less fraud protection," etc.
2. Get the server running and connected to a chat client (see [[SIMPLE - how to use]]).
3. Say the tradeoff in plain English — don't try to guess official parameter names, the semantic search tools handle that.
4. You'll get back a named strategy category (e.g. "Segmentation," "Do It in Reverse") and maybe a generic engineering example.
5. Treat that strategy as a brainstorming prompt, not an answer. Ask "what would Segmentation look like for our onboarding flow?" and let the team riff from there.

Don't expect a finished feature out of this — you get a *direction*, not a spec. The value is forcing your team to name the real tradeoff clearly and giving you an unfamiliar angle to brainstorm from, faster than arguing from scratch.
