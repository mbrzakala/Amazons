# Validator — 2. Persona

Checks: `Hackathon Wiki/stages/1-discovery/steps/2-persona.md`.

## What this step is proving

Exactly one specific, non-generic target user exists, and their pain point actually
connects to the task-1 idea — not a committee of personas, not a placeholder ("a busy
user").

## Evidence to gather

- The recorded persona (name, role/context, goal, pain point) — step's `## Result`
  section or team notes.
- The task-1 idea sentence, to cross-check relevance.

## Gate checklist

1. **Exactly one persona is defined, not several.**
   Evidence: count of personas recorded. Fail if more than one candidate persona was
   left un-chosen.
2. **Name, role/context, goal, and pain point are all filled in with specifics.**
   Evidence: the four fields. Fail if any field is generic/interchangeable (e.g. "a busy
   user," "wants a better experience").
3. **The pain point is relevant to the idea from task 1.**
   Evidence: compare the pain point against the task-1 sentence. Fail if there's no
   traceable connection.

See `agent_doc/README.md` for the verdict scale and reporting format.
