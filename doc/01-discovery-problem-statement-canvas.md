# Problem Statement Canvas

User: R&D scientist at an international humanitarian organization (UNICEF/UN/WHO)

> **Humanitarian R&D scientists** have a problem with **energy-intensive and fragile desalination systems** when **providing clean water to off-grid, drought-stricken communities**.
> This causes them to feel **deeply frustrated** and lose **vital budget due to sudden 70% system efficiency drops**.
> Currently, they cope by **relying on diesel-powered purification and water trucking**, which is ineffective because **it is economically and logistically unsustainable**.


## CONTEXT (Kontekst)

* **Situation:** The user is deploying emergency water-relief systems in arid coastal regions where traditional freshwater sources (aquifers, rivers) have completely dried up.
* **Environment:** A high-stakes humanitarian crisis plagued by extreme heat, a crumbling or non-existent power grid, and soaring fuel logistics costs. Time is running out as local groundwater disappears and geopolitical pressure to deliver low-cost tech mounts.


## PROBLEM (Problem)

* **Core Difficulty:** Current desalination technologies are "energy vampires" that suffer from rapid equipment degradation (scaling, membrane fouling, and corrosion).
* **Impact:** This high energy demand and frequent need for specialized maintenance make modern desalination economically impossible and operationally unsustainable in the world's poorest regions.


## CUSTOMER / USER (Klient / Użytkownik)

* **Target Profile:** R&D scientists, field engineers, and technical advisors working within global humanitarian agencies (UN, WHO, UNICEF).
* **Key Characteristics:** Highly educated and data-driven, yet deeply pragmatic; they value robust, low-maintenance, and "plug-and-play" solutions that can survive harsh, resource-constrained field conditions.


## ALTERNATIVES (Alternatywy)

* **Current Workarounds:** Organizations rely on standard Reverse Osmosis (RO) units powered by expensive, polluting diesel generators, or resort to trucking in water via costly tankers.
* **Limitations:** Smaller-scale alternatives, like basic solar stills, are eco-friendly but suffer from dangerously low throughput that cannot sustain growing populations.


## EMOTIONAL IMPACT (Wpływ Emocjonalny)

* **User's Feelings:** The scientist feels **deeply frustrated** by the gap between theoretical laboratory breakthroughs and rugged, real-world deployment.
* **Pressure:** They experience intense anxiety and moral weight, knowing that engineering bottlenecks and high operational costs are directly delaying life-saving water delivery.


## MEASURABLE GAP (Mierzalna Luka)

* **Quantifiable Metrics:** A stark 50–70% drop in desalination system efficiency within months due to scaling, combined with energy costs that consume up to 40% of a local relief budget.
* **The Gap:** The critical delta between the current $3–4 \text{ kWh/m}^3$ energy requirement of standard RO and the necessary $<1 \text{ kWh/m}^3$ threshold needed for off-grid, sustainable operation.

# Persona

> **David Holz, Systems Architect**


## PROFILE / BIO (Profil / Bio)

David is a Senior Systems & Solution Architect working within a global humanitarian R&D lab. Professionally, he is responsible for designing macro-level, scalable infrastructure for crisis zones, while privately he is a tech-sustainability enthusiast. He bridges the gap between high-level organizational planning and the gritty reality of field deployments.


## GOALS & MOTIVATIONS (Cele I Motywacje)

* **Systemic Impact:** He aims to architect a decentralized, off-grid desalination framework that slashes energy dependencies below **1 kWh/m³**.
* **Sustainability:** He is highly motivated to transition emergency water relief from temporary, expensive "band-aid" fixes to self-sustaining, long-term infrastructure.
* **Efficiency:** He wants to prove that eco-friendly solutions can match or exceed the output of traditional, heavy-industrial methods.


## PAINS & FRUSTRATIONS (Bóle i Frustracje)

* **The Theory-Reality Gap:** He is deeply frustrated by "paper-perfect" academic technologies that instantly fail or clog up when exposed to harsh field environments.
* **Resource Drain:** David wastes immense time and budget dealing with constant maintenance bottlenecks and the staggering logistical costs of diesel generators.
* **Bureaucratic Pressure:** He feels stuck between rigid organizational budgets and the immediate, life-or-death pressure of water scarcity.


## BEHAVIORS & HABITS (Zachowania i Nawyki)

* **Data-Driven Evaluation:** On a daily basis, he relies on data-modeling software, system architecture diagrams, and field telemetry data to assess infrastructure performance.
* **Knowledge Seeking:** He stays informed by auditing technical whitepapers, attending international engineering committees, and reviewing peer-reviewed sustainability journals.
* **Collaborative Mindset:** He acts as a gatekeeper, regularly coordinating with both field engineers on the ground and executive decision-makers at headquarters.


## CONTEXT OF USE (Kontekst Użycia)

David will interact with your solution at his office desk while evaluating technical proposals for the next major regional deployment cycle, or on a rugged laptop during a rapid field assessment. He needs a comprehensive system overview that instantly proves its thermodynamic efficiency and long-term mechanical viability before he risks organizational budget on it.


## VOICE OF USER (Cytat)

> "I don't need another over-engineered lab experiment; I need a robust system architecture that can actually survive the field without a constant supply of diesel and spare parts."
